There are 4 blocks for different categories:

The first and most junior category is Montessory Wing.In this group there are 3 classes that are further divided into 8 sections. Each class has:
Low, open shelves with self-correcting materials (practical life, sensorial, language, math) Child-sized tables/chairs arranged in small work areas and floor work mats Natural light, neutral colors, minimal wall clutter, labeled baskets/trays Practical-life corner (pouring, spooning, dressing frames), reading nook, and peace/calm area Display at child eye-level, lots of hands-on materials vs. posters

Then the Primary wing. This wing has classes from 1-5.These classes are also divided into another 8 sections. Teaching Style & Activities There’s also an emphasis on speaking and listening: for example, children are encouraged to retell stories, record themselves, and practice speaking with expression. Writing skills are practiced through neat handwriting, diary entries, and more. For subjects like Mathematics and Creative Work, the Class I worksheet requires “colorful booklets,” charts, poster making, and models. Curriculum & Subject Focus In primary classes, they cover core subjects like English, Mathematics, Urdu, and non-core like Islamiyat, General Science, Social Studies, Arts & Craft, and Computer.
